Alexandre Delasalle

(he/him) is a visual artist and designer based in Paris, working between France and the Netherlands.

His practice focuses on objects, installations, and furniture with an emphasis on imagery and narrative. He works across scenography, interior design, visual research, and editorial design.
His work combines industrial production methods with more informal and amateur approaches. Drawing from natural and floral imagery, aesthetics of “cuteness,” and references from popular culture, he develops a visual language based on mimicry, used to question contemporary aesthetic standards.
Alongside his independent practice, he collaborates with institutions and creative clients on projects in scenography, interior design, and visual communication.
